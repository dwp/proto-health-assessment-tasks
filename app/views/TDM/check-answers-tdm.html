Daily living

Preparing food

Functional history for preparing food

Change

She is unable to prepare and cook a simple meal from fresh ingredients for 1 person. Her concentration is quite poor and tends to forget switching electric appliances on and off regularly. There have been instances when she's had accidents in the kitchen due to accidently burning herself. She is unable to cut, peel and chop ingredients or open up packaging, tins and jars due to her hand pans.

Taking nutrition

Functional history for taking nutrition

Change

Whenever she has a plate of food in front of her, she can independently convey it to the mouth, chew and swallow, reporting no regular spillage episodes. She'd need to be reminded to eat regularly, and once reminded she'd get herself to eat something. She normally eats 1 main meal daily, tending to snack on fruit throughout the day when she feels like.

Managing therapy

Functional history for managing therapy

Change

She tends to be forgetful about which medication she has to take at which time of day. Normally certain symptoms come on, and this would serve as a reminder to her that she needs to take her tablets. She is able to manage pill packets and can administer the tablets with a glass of water independently. She currently has a monthly prescription. She is in control of her medication and tablets are at her disposal.

Washing and bathing

Functional history for washing and bathing

Change

She needs to hold onto the grab rails at the side of the bath to make bathing transfers safely owing to added leverage and stability required due to her physical pains.

Managing toilet needs

Functional history for managing toilet needs

Change

She holds onto the nearby sink and the walking stick for added leverage and support to steadily get on and off the toilet due to her physical pains. Otherwise she can attend to her hygiene needs and can maintain hygiene and cleanliness throughout without difficulties.

Dressing and undressing

Functional history for dressing and undressing

Change

She is physically capable of putting on and taking off t-shirts, shirts, sweaters and tops independently without difficulties. She tends to find managing zips, fastenings and buttons a bit fiddly due to her hand pains and possibly having swollen fingers. She needs to be sitting down to put on and take off trousers, socks and shoes, for lower half items once sat down she leans forwards to reach down to her feet and lifts her knees closer to her chest in sitting, depending on which position is mostly comfortable.

Communicating verbally

Functional history for communicating verbally

Change

She does not express any significant issues with verbal communication, speaking and hearing. Normally she can speak to people, can understand what is being said and can maintain a conversation going without difficulties.

Reading and understanding signs, symbols and words

She can easily distinguish between signs and symbols in roads and public places.

Engaging face to face

Mobility

Planning and following journeys

Functional history for planning and following journeys

Change

She usually gets a lift to attend for appointments, as she's used to it being done this way. She usually goes out to the countryside accompanied by her friend once every couple of weeks.

Moving around

Functional history for moving around

Change

She normally feels tired and in pain after covering such distances despite trying to walk as tolerated. She normally walks at a slow, comfortable pace, aided with a walking stick at all times for added leverage, support and stability. She doesn't use wheelchairs or mobility scooters for outdoors mobility. She's had some falls over this past year due to legs giving way unpredictably and being really clumsy due to unsteadiness. She isn't a blue badge holder. Her walking pattern is described as slow and at times wobbly. Indoors - she can access all rooms downstairs but if the pain is worse she's unable to manage stairs and sleeps downstairs on the settee.
