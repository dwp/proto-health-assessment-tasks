Daily living

Preparing food

Functional history for preparing food

She is unable to prepare and cook a simple meal from fresh ingredients for 1 person. Her concentration is quite poor and tends to forget switching electric appliances on and off regularly. There have been instances when she's had accidents in the kitchen due to accidently burning herself. She is unable to cut, peel and chop ingredients or open up packaging, tins and jars due to her hand pans.

Taking nutrition

Functional history for taking nutrition

Whenever she has a plate of food in front of her, she can independently convey it to the mouth, chew and swallow, reporting no regular spillage episodes. However she generally eats foods which require her to only use a fork or a spoon with the right hand for ease and convenience purposes as her left hand might cause tremors.

Managing therapy

Functional history for managing therapy

She tends to be forgetful about which medication she has to take at which time of day. Normally certain symptoms come on, and this would serve as a reminder to her that she needs to take her tablets. her behalf should any sudden changes to her conditions occur, but she can also not contact anyone at all as she prefers to let the sensation pass.

Washing and bathing

Functional history for washing and bathing

She needs to hold onto the grab rails at the side of the bath to make bathing transfers safely owing to added leverage and stability required due to her physical pains. Normally she has a shower, she sits at the edge of the bath whilst holding onto the grab rails to get in the bath, she then stands in the bath to shower, and whilst standing in the bath she can independently wash her hair, upper body and lower body.

Managing toilet needs

Functional history for managing toilet needs

She holds onto the nearby sink and the walking stick for added leverage and support to steadily get on and off the toilet due to her physical pains. Otherwise she can attend to her hygiene needs and can maintain hygiene and cleanliness throughout without difficulties.

Dressing and undressing

Functional history for dressing and undressing

She is physically capable of putting on and taking off t-shirts, shirts, sweaters and tops independently without difficulties. She tends to find managing zips, fastenings and buttons a bit fiddly due to her hand pains and possibly having swollen fingers. She needs to be sitting down to put on and take off trousers, socks and shoes, for lower half items once sat down she leans forwards to reach down to her feet and lifts her knees closer to her chest in sitting, depending on which position is mostly comfortable.

Communicating verbally

Functional history for communicating verbally

She does not express any significant issues with verbal communication, speaking and hearing. Normally she can speak to people, can understand what is being said and can maintain a conversation going without difficulties.

Reading and understanding signs, symbols and words

Functional history for reading and understanding signs, symbols and words

She is able to read and understand basic and complex written information and sentences without difficulties. If she receives official documentation, letters and correspondence she wouldn't require complex information to be broken down into simple text for her to make sense out of what she's just read. She can easily distinguish between signs and symbols in roads and public places.

Engaging face to face

Functional history for engaging face to face

If she is to interact with a new person face to face, she'd need to be notified of the meeting beforehand so that she mentally prepares herself for the actual encounter, and she would also need any friend or close relative support her before, during and after she meets that person to ensure that she remains calm and composed.

Budgeting

Functional history for budgeting

She has a good understanding of money and is aware of how money works. She is aware of the concept of money, the value of things and the need to budget for future purchases.



Mobility

Planning and following journeys

Functional history for planning and following journeys

She usually gets a lift to attend for appointments, as she's used to it being done this way. She usually goes out to the countryside accompanied by her friend once every couple of weeks.

Moving around

Functional history for moving around

She can comfortably cover up to 30 metres prior to needing to stop, it would approximately take her 5 minutes to walk such distance, and she'd need to stop owing to knee pains especially in the left knee.
